To aspirate a consonant, add an h after it (e.g. phot “pot”). Aspirated consonants are unaspirated word-finally.

Ğ is pronounced [w] after round/flat vowels, and [j] after thin vowels.

Double consonants are held longer (e.g. onne vs one).

The g and k in ng/nk are pronounced and join the next syllable.

b, d, g soften between vowels to [β ð ɣ].

t and d before y or i become [t͡ɕ] and [d͡ʑ].

Vowel Harmony

Bolankian vowels are divided into round, flat, and thin groups. Suffix vowels harmonize with the root vowel category.

Round Vowels

a, u, o, ö, ä, ou, au

Flat Vowels

ë, eu, ı, ï, oi

Thin Vowels

e, i, é, ei, ai, ü

Phonotactics

Bolankian syllables follow (C)(C)V(C)(C). Up to two consonants may occur at the start or end.

Onset Rules

  • stop + liquid/glide
  • sibilant + stop
  • sibilant + stop + liquid/glide
  • consonant + y

Coda Rules

  • nasal + stop
  • liquid/glide + stop
  • nasal + fricative
  • liquid/glide + fricative
  • stop + stop (kt, kd, pt, bd, etc.)

Stress

Stress typically falls on the first syllable of a root unless modified:

  1. Carated vowels receive stress
  2. If multiple, stress the first
  3. Otherwise, stress diphthongs
  4. If multiple, stress the first
  5. Otherwise, stress the first syllable

Morphology

Bolankian is an agglutinative language, meaning it forms words and expresses grammatical relationships by adding a series of affixes to a root word. Each affix typically represents a single grammatical function, allowing for clear and systematic word formation.

Affixes in Bolankian are primarily prefixes and suffixes, but changes may also be made to the root for better speaking flow.

Noun Morphology

Number

Number in Bolankian is a prefix. It includes singular, plural, number, dual, and absence.

  • Singular: no marker
  • Plural: la- / le-
  • Number: written like the number
  • Dual: ïla- / ïle-
  • Absence: yos-

Examples:

Surface Form lag'on
Segmented Form la-g'on
Gloss PL-book
Translation "books"
Surface Form ïlejenik
Segmented Form ïle-jenik
Gloss DU-flower
Translation "two flowers"
Surface Form yoskadeğs
Segmented Form yoskadeğs
Gloss ABS-house
Translation "no house"

Case

Bolankian uses suffix case markers to indicate the grammatical roles of nouns in sentences.

  • Nominative (subject): no marker
  • Accusative (direct object): -leut / -eut
  • Dative (indirect object): -on / -en / -ğon / -ğen / -n
  • Genitive (possession): -nın / -onan / -nin / -ünin
  • Locative (location): -da / -de / -şa / -şe / -ça / -çe
  • Ablative (movement away): -dan / -den / -şan / -şen / -çan / -çen
  • Instrumental (means): -le / -e / -yla / -ye / -la / -a

Examples:

Surface Form kidevye
Segmented Form kidev-ye
Gloss stick-INS
Translation "with a stick"
Surface Form lajandogaleut
Segmented Form la-jandoga-leut
Gloss PL-woman-ACC
Translation "women (as direct object)"

Diminution

Bolankian uses prefixes to indicate diminution (smallness or endearment) in nouns.

  • Diminutive: -cik / -çak
  • Augmentative: -büğik / -boyïk

Examples:

Surface Form çakjandoga
Segmented Form çak-jandoga
Gloss DIM-woman
Translation "girl"
Surface Form boyïkadeğs
Segmented Form boyïk-kadeğs
Gloss AUG-house
Translation "mansion"

Articles

Bolankian uses prefixes to indicate definiteness and indefiniteness in nouns.

  • Definite Article: aglo- / agle-
  • Indefinite Article: aña- / añe-

Examples:

Surface Form lojanage
Segmented Form lo-janage
Gloss ART.DEF-man[SG]
Translation "the man"
Surface Form añalakadeğs
Segmented Form aña-la-kadeğs
Gloss ART.INDEF-PL-house
Translation "some houses"

If a noun is possessed by a pronoun, Bolankian uses a suffix.

Verb Morphology

Bolankian does not have true verbs. Instead, most verbal expressions are denominal constructions, or nouns that are changed into a verbal meaning. This is accomplished with the denominalizing suffix, -ap / -ep, followed by an auxiliary particle if needed.

Example:

Surface Form suğapeul
Segmented Form su-ğap-eul
Gloss water-DENOM-have
Translation "to drink" (literally "having water")
Surface Form yemepeul
Segmented Form yem-ep-eul
Gloss food-DENOM-have
Translation "to eat" (literally "having food")

-eul in this example is an auxiliary particle meaning "to have"
